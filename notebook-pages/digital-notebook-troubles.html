Man we sure do love free tiers

On November 4th, Bryson received the following email from Notion

They were telling us that we had nearly run out of "blocks" in our Notion workspace.
A "block" is the unit used whenever we hit enter and add a paragraph, or a heading, or an embed.
While this email was completely incorrect about how many blocks we had left, it brought up a concern about possibly hitting this limit later on in the season.
When we set up the Notion earlier this season, we thought that blocks were unlimited, but that turned out to only be for individual users, not for teams (like ours).

On November 14th, Bryson began converting Notion pages over to Obsidian

Obsidian is an self hostable alternative to Notion (FREE!!). It primarily uses Markdown to format notes, and saves all notes in an open format easily utilized in many ways.
While Obsidian stock lacks many of the features Notion had and proudly advertised, it is heavily extensible through its library of plugins and themes. Using these, we can nearly mimic Notion in many ways, all while not having to worry about paying.

Key Differences with us using Notion

  • Desktop App - Obsidian is only available in the desktop app. We no longer have the versability of having our notes available after a quick Log In with Google on any computer.
  • Significantly nicer export workflow. Notion locks many of the nice export features behind a paywall
  • Significantly more customizable
  • Local Storage
    • Obsidian saves all notes locally. This is dangerous and prone to corruption and losing our entire notebook. To prevent this we are using Box as backup storage. Using their desktop app, our cloud storage account is mounted as a drive within Windows and we can use Obsidian with a cloud backup.
    • Box has a 10gb free limit which is far more than we will ever hit.
    • Bryson chose Box over other more common alternatives because many of the alternatives won't allow signing into multiple accounts at the same time, and he had a feeling that no one else would have a Box account considering its a much smaller competitor compared to the others.
  • Embed experience isn't AS streamlined.
    • Still possible using HTML and a little bit of critical thinking, but more challenging than Notion
