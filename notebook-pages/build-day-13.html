Today we continued working on our elevation system. We spent some time requesting more high strength shaft parts as we noticed that we don't have very many right now. Our prototype will likely have enough shaft collars, but we will need more if we plan on using these more.
We eventually finished up the elevator, and here are our thoughts:

The Roller Elevator




We thought that this design was a good idea, but had some serious fatal flaws

Unbalanced

Using this design, it is critical that everything is incredibly well balanced in order for this to work. Down the road, if we changed something on our robot and increased the weight of it somewhat significantly, it could completely screw up the balance of the lift, and mess up the balance of our elevator. This was apparent even in our small prototype that we built.
Is this a risk worth taking?

A Lot of Power

We attached one motor to this lift, thinking that that would be enough for it to lift itself somewhat with basically no weight.
The prototype didn't move at all.
If one motor can't move it with NO weight, can 4 with the weight of an entire robot, plus stay locked when power cuts?

Narrow

It was difficult for us to line this up by hand, much less while driving in the middle of a match.

Other Options

We talked again about the potential of using a scissor lift, as that design is very reliable and strong when going straight up and down when built correctly. This option should be tabled for the future, so we can talk about it as a team when Ben and Chloe are back.
